Space Time:

Spacetime is the four-dimensional fabric that combines the three dimensions of space with the dimension of time. According to Albert Einstein's theory of relativity, space and time are not separate but are connected and can be affected by matter and energy. Massive objects such as stars, planets, and black holes bend spacetime around them, and this bending is what we experience as gravity.

The idea of spacetime is needed to explain many phenomena that cannot be fully understood using the older view of space and time as separate things. For example, it helps explain why time passes more slowly near massive objects and why light bends when it passes close to a star. Spacetime is also essential for understanding black holes, the expansion of the universe, and the motion of planets and galaxies.

Scientists have observed the effects of spacetime through many experiments and observations. One famous example is the bending of starlight during a solar eclipse, which confirmed Einstein's predictions. More recently, scientists have detected gravitational waves, which are ripples in spacetime caused by powerful events such as colliding black holes. These discoveries provide strong evidence that spacetime is a real feature of our universe.
